Every conversation starts from zero.

AI assistants are powerful. But they don't know you. They never will — unless we fix the architecture.

Stateless by design

Cursor, ChatGPT, Claude — every session resets. Your preferences, your patterns, your context: gone.

Memory is just notes

Mem0, Supermemory, Letta — they store what you said. But reading about you isn't the same as knowing you.

Sessions, not relationships

AI assistants are powerful tools. But tools don't grow with you. Relationships do.

Three layers. One presence.

Quarq uses a three-tier memory architecture — each layer optimized for what it stores best.

Behavioral Layer·Model Weights (SLM)Continual LoRA fine-tuning

Your communication style, decision patterns, and implicit preferences — baked directly into the model. No retrieval needed. The model just is this way.

Knowledge Layer·Memory DatabaseSemantic knowledge graph

Facts, events, relationships, documents. Structured and searchable. Fast tier for recent access, slow tier for deep history.

Working Memory·Context WindowReal-time

The active session — current task, pointers to what matters now. Small, focused, purposeful.

The SLM routes between all three — because it knows you.
